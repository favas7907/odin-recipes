Biryani

Description

Biryani is a rich and aromatic rice dish made with basmati rice, spices, and meat such as chicken, beef, or mutton. It is layered and cooked slowly to allow the flavors to blend perfectly.

Originating from the Indian subcontinent, biryani is known for its vibrant taste and fragrant spices. It is often served with raita or salad and is a favorite for special occasions and celebrations.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500g chicken or meat
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste
  • Whole spices (bay leaf, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil or ghee

Steps

  1. Wash and soak the basmati rice for 30 minutes.
  2. Cook the rice until it is 70% done and set aside.
  3. Heat oil or ghee and fry the sliced onions until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste, tomatoes, and spices; cook well.
  5. Add the meat and yogurt, then cook until the meat is tender.
  6. Layer the partially cooked rice over the meat mixture.
  7. Sprinkle coriander and mint leaves on top.
  8. Cover and cook on low heat (dum) for 15–20 minutes.
  9. Serve hot with raita or salad.
