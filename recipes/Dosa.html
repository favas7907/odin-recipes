Masala Dosa

Description

Masala Dosa is a popular South Indian dish made from a fermented rice and urad dal batter, spread thin and crisp on a hot griddle. It is filled with a spiced potato mixture and served hot, often accompanied by coconut chutney and sambar.

Known for its crispy texture and flavorful filling, Masala Dosa is a staple breakfast item across India. It combines a slightly tangy dosa with a savory potato masala, making it both delicious and satisfying.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rice
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil or ghee for cooking
  • 3–4 boiled potatoes
  • 1 onion, finely sliced
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste (for filling)

Steps

  1. Soak rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds for 6–8 hours.
  2. Grind them into a smooth batter and allow it to ferment overnight.
  3. Heat a pan and spread a ladle of batter into a thin circle.
  4. Drizzle oil or ghee and cook until the dosa turns crispy and golden.
  5. For the filling, heat oil and add mustard seeds and curry leaves.
  6. Add onions and green chilies, sauté until soft.
  7. Add turmeric powder and mashed potatoes, mix well and cook for a few minutes.
  8. Place the potato filling inside the dosa, fold, and serve hot.
