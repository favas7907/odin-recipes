Beef Fry

Description

Beef Fry is a popular South Indian, especially Kerala-style, dish made by cooking beef with spices and roasting it until dark and flavorful. It is known for its rich taste and slightly crispy texture.

This dish is often served with parotta, rice, or tapioca. The use of coconut slices and curry leaves gives it a distinct aroma and authentic Kerala flavor.

Ingredients

  • 500g beef, cleaned and cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Few curry leaves
  • 1/2 cup coconut slices
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 tablespoons coconut oil

Steps

  1. Marinate the beef with turmeric, chili powder, salt, and ginger-garlic paste.
  2. Pressure cook the beef until tender.
  3. Heat coconut oil in a pan and add curry leaves.
  4. Add onions and green chilies, sauté until golden.
  5. Add cooked beef and cook on medium heat.
  6. Add black pepper and garam masala.
  7. Add coconut slices and fry until the beef becomes dark and slightly crispy.
  8. Serve hot with parotta or rice.
